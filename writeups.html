CTF Writeups

CTF competitions are the "hackathons" of computer security enthusiasts. Solving problems in CTFs is the best way to keep your knowledge relevant and sharp.
Einstein once said that "If you can't explain [something] simply, you don't understand it well enough." To that end, I will attempt to amalgamate my various CTF experiences here and delineate the exact steps taken to arrive at solutions. They not only serves as a great reference for myself in the future but also will help others learn. I love teaching others about what I know, and am always searching for the best solutions. If you notice anything that is incorrect or could be improved, please don't hesitate to let me know!

# To find the plaintext, just do c^d mod n.

# Convert to hex and remove the "0x" from the beginning as well as the "L" from the end
